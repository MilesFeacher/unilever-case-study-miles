## 1. Leadership & Organizational Structure

### CEO: Fernando Fernandez (In role ~14 months as of Dec 2025)

**Key Context**:
- 37 years at Unilever ("My 37 years in Unilever is an advantage when driving change")
- Leadership philosophy: Clear prioritization on "volume growth, positive mix, consistent gross margin expansion, profit growth in hard currency"
- Strategic focus: "Heavy lifting of organisational change is behind us. Now it's about continually elevating brand quality and execution."
- Obsession: **Premiumisation** ("I'm obsessed with premiumisation")

**Interview Date**: December 12, 2025 (JP Morgan Consumer Staples Conference)

**Major Strategic Initiatives Under Fernando**:
1. **Portfolio Transformation** ("Fewer, Bigger, Better" strategy implicit):
   - Food business sale to McCormick & Co. (announced March 2026, ~$44.8B deal)
   - Rationale: Focus on faster-growth Beauty & Wellbeing and Personal Care segments
   - Acquisitions in high-growth areas: Liquid I.V. ($120M→$1B in 5 years), Nutrafol ($220M→~$1B)

2. **SASSY Framework** (Product development and marketing integration):
   - **S**: Science (superior functionality)
   - **A**: Aesthetics (consumer preference)
   - **S**: Sensorials (scent, touch, neurological signals)
   - **S**: Said by Others (social/influencer validation)
   - **Y**: Young-spirited brands (culturally resonant)

3. **Gross Margin Expansion Strategy** (4 levers):
   - Volume growth (target: +2% company-level growth)
   - Category/segment/channel mix optimization
   - Value chain interventions (€1.3B investment in fragrances)
   - CapEx allocation: ~60% to margin expansion initiatives

  - Plumbing = systems, data, AI capabilities
- **Key capabilities**:
  - AI models predicting trends before they happen

## 3. Specific AI Implementation Details

### A. Hefei, China: Factory-to-Consumer Distribution Centre

**WEF Global Lighthouse Network Status**: Supply Chain Resilience Distinction (2026)

**Operational Model**:
- Transformed from traditional manufacturing to factory-to-consumer (direct-to-e-commerce) fulfillment
- Serving d-commerce (digital commerce) demand across China
- Dedicated e-commerce distribution centre fulfilling orders directly from factory

**Technology Stack**:
- **GenAI applications**: 65% of 31 digitally enabled use cases
- **Robotics**: ~120 robots deployed for goods movement
- **Automation level**: 90% (highest for FMCG distribution)
- **AI supply chain management**: Real-time supply chain optimization (GenAI)
- **System integration**: Linking 12+ systems across partners and online stores

**Business Impact** (Verified):
| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Order fulfillment speed | 75% faster |
| Logistics costs | Down 24% |
| Inventory capacity expansion | 50% without expanding footprint |
| Daily order volume | 31,000+ orders/day across 400+ products |
| Scope | First FMCG distribution centre to earn WEF Lighthouse status |

**Scaling**: Factory-to-consumer model and solutions rolled out to all Unilever sites in China; now global benchmark.

---

### B. Gandhidham, India: Personal Care Manufacturing

**WEF Global Lighthouse Network Status**: Sustainability Distinction (2026)

**Location Context**: Kutch region, water-scarce environment (200+ days drought historically)

**Technology Deployment**:
- **AI and digital twins**: Reducing on-site water consumption and supporting local farmers to do the same
- **High-speed cameras + machine learning**: Spot defects, cut waste, ensure quality
- **Digital twins**: Optimize energy use, support transition to renewable fuels and low-emission refrigerants
- **Product portfolio**: Power Brands (Lux, Lifebuoy, Dove)

**Business & Sustainability Impact** (Verified):
| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| On-site water use | 17% reduction |
| Community water saved | 6+ billion litres |
| Material waste reduction | 48% less |
| Scope 1 & 2 emissions | 90% reduction |
| Production growth (2021-2024) | Supported 24% production increase |

**Community Impact**: AI digital twins assisting local farmers in water stewardship (implicit in "supporting local farmers to do the same").

### C. Pondicherry, India: Home Care Manufacturing

**WEF Global Lighthouse Network Status**: Productivity Distinction (2026)

**Operational Context**:
- Largest Home Care site for South Asia
- Manufactures Power Brands: Surf Excel, Rin, Vim, Comfort
- Supplies 60% of regional (South India) demand (up from 40%)

**Technology Deployment**:
- **Machine learning**: Predicts and controls product quality in real time
- **AI cameras**: Identify and fix line issues, reducing downtime
- **Digital tools**: Enable flexible production (3x product variety without space expansion)

**Business Impact** (Verified):
| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Production speed | Doubled |
| Production volume | Increased by 25% |
| Product variants | 3x increase within existing capacity |
| Regional supply | 60% of South India (up from 40%) |

### D. R&D AI: "Highest Concentration of Robots Doing Material Chemistry"

**Source**: Dr Samantha Samaras, SVP Science & Technology, Personal Care (April 2, 2026)

**Capabilities**:
- AI shifting R&D "from observation to prediction"
- Model biological systems, simulate outcomes, design products with greater precision
- Run thousands of controlled experiments in parallel
- Acceleration: What took months can now be done in days

**Scientific Breakthroughs Enabled by AI**:
1. **Skin microbiome research**:
   - Analyzed 30,000+ samples
   - Own more than half the world's skin microbiome data
   - Discovered link between microbiome and psychological wellbeing (published)

2. **Product applications**:
   - Lux Magic Orchid shower gel: Scent triggers confidence response in 100 milliseconds (faster than conscious thought)
   - Olly mood-boosting body washes: Using neurosignalling insights to create emotion-targeted formulations
   - Dove Scalp + Hair Therapy: Addressing scalp health as foundation for confidence/self-esteem

3. **Framework**: "Desire at Scale" — products that work with biology to create desire, performance, growth
