## Case Study Quality
This case centers on Sam Kini, Unilever's Chief Digital and Technology Officer, as she decides how to sequence Unilever's AI-enabled transformation. The core initiative is the five-year Google Cloud partnership, which is meant to build an AI-first digital backbone for marketing, commerce, and data integration.

The strategic question is not whether Unilever should adopt AI, but how it should move first. A commercialization-first approach promised visible wins but risked technical debt; a platform-first approach improved governance but could look too slow; a federated dual-track model tried to balance both, but only if coordination stayed tight. The case was strong because the tradeoffs were real and none of the options was obviously correct.

## Quality Verification
I checked the case against the annual report, the Q4 2025 transcript, Unilever leadership materials, third-party articles in the source registry, the verification log, and the verification-debt tracker. The key check was making sure the Google Cloud deal was treated as a documented five-year partnership, not just a generic AI narrative.

I caught and corrected several AI errors. I kept agentic shopping caveated as strategic intent rather than scaled deployment, removed unsupported claims such as 23,000 employees trained on GenAI, 500+ AI projects, and "go wide and go deep," and avoided asserting a formal CEO appointment date that was not fully sourced. Those corrections mattered because they separated evidence from plausible-sounding inference.

## Reflection & Learning
I learned that AI tools work best as a research-and-drafting system, not as a substitute for evidence. The strongest part of the workflow was iterating between source capture, verification, and rewriting. Third-party coverage from Computer Weekly and Consumer Goods Technology helped confirm that the partnership was being read as an operating-model shift, not just a vendor announcement.

I also learned that Unilever's transformation is bigger than one cloud deal. The company already had evidence of AI execution in Sketch Pro, supply chain visibility, and Lighthouse sites. The Google Cloud partnership sits inside that broader pattern of connecting marketing, commerce, data, and operations into one system.

## AI Tool Usage & Process
I used VS Code with GitHub Copilot, browser-based research, and repository edits together. The best workflow was source first, draft second, verify third, revise fourth. Prompts worked best when they were specific about the decision question and the evidence required.

What worked especially well was using multiple tools in sequence: search for sources, capture excerpts, update the source registry, then revise the case documents. If I did this again, I would start with even tighter source scoping before drafting and keep the verification log open earlier so unsupported claims never enter the narrative in the first place.
